Leon's Story

He's been where your child is — and he found a way through.

Leon has spent the last decade marking O-Level Chinese papers — reading thousands of scripts, seeing exactly where students lose marks, and understanding what examiners actually reward. But before he ever sat on that side of the table, he was on the other side: the student who thought Chinese was impossible.

Leon grew up speaking English at home — Chinese never came naturally. He was scolded by teachers for it as a child. He worked hard, did "well enough" on paper, but never felt confident. At JC1, he scraped a C5. At A-Levels, a D. He was devastated — not because he hadn't tried, but because he'd hit a ceiling and didn't know how to break through it. When MOE offered him the chance to teach Math instead, the easier path, he made a choice: he would do Chinese anyway.

He enrolled in Chinese Studies at NTU, earned an MOE Teaching Award scholarship, and in his third year, went on exchange to Peking University. For the first two weeks, he couldn't speak. Not because he didn't know the language, but because the fear of getting it wrong was paralyzing. Eventually, he found his voice — through immersion, through courage, and by learning how he learned best. That breakthrough became the foundation of how he teaches today.

Now, with 17 years teaching across MOE schools, tuition centres — including as a Director of Chinese Curriculum — and 1-to-1 settings, Leon teaches with both perspectives: he knows what struggling students feel, and he knows exactly what examiners want. That combination is rare — and it's why students who felt stuck start seeing Chinese as manageable.

The Approach

Strategy, not
memorisation.

Leon uses English strategically — to explain difficult concepts, break down confusion, and make the abstract concrete. If your child can follow in Chinese, he'll teach primarily in Chinese. Either way, all notes are bilingual, and the focus is always on clarity, not perfection.

01

First, we make sense of it all.

The first 4–5 sessions are about making Chinese feel manageable — not overwhelming. Leon explains everything in a way your child actually understands, using English when needed. By the end, they know exactly what's expected of them.

02

Then, we go after the marks that are there for the taking.

Email Writing, Composition, and Oral can all be prepared for — and scored in — without perfect language. Leon's templates show students exactly what to write, in simple sentences, to get marks. Quick wins come first.

03

Then, we keep practising until it sticks.

Real past exam questions. Personalised feedback every session. Gradual confidence. For students who want to go further, Leon layers in reading and vocabulary work to close the gaps in their foundation.

Leon's core principle: content is king, not perfect language. You don't need flawless Chinese to score — you need clear, structured sentences that hit the marking criteria. That's what his templates are built around.

Students also receive Leon's proprietary O-Level guidebook and practice workbooks — built from 17 years of teaching and 10 years of marking. 1-to-1 students get a customised digital copy tailored to their needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Good questions.
Straight answers.

Do you take other levels — P6, Lower Sec, JC?+
Leon's core focus is Sec 3 and Sec 4 students preparing for O-Levels. Other levels — including P6, Lower Secondary, and JC H1 — are taken on selectively and assessed case by case. WhatsApp to have that conversation.
Do you offer online lessons?+
Yes. Lessons can be conducted online, at your home, or at Leon's place in Boon Lay. The format is flexible — what matters is that the session works for your child.
How does the trial work?+
The trial is a single 1-hour session at the standard hourly rate. Leon will conduct a diagnostic assessment of your child's current level, share one key strategy to address their most pressing challenge, and discuss realistic goals. At the end, both sides decide honestly whether it's a good fit before any commitment is made.
What materials do students get?+
All students receive Leon's proprietary O-Level guidebook and practice workbooks, developed over 17 years of teaching and 10 years of marking. Students on a 1-to-1 arrangement receive a customised digital copy tailored specifically to their needs and weak areas.
How often are lessons, and what's the cancellation policy?+
Lessons are held once a week at a fixed recurring timeslot. This consistency is important for progress. Cancellations are accepted with at least 24 hours' notice and a valid reason — otherwise, the session is considered taken and the cancellation policy applies.
My child is really weak. Can Leon still help?+
It depends on foundation and attitude. Very weak students can and do improve — but the timeline needs to be realistic. A student at F9 aiming for a pass may need one to two years of consistent work. Leon will be upfront about this at the trial rather than overpromise.
What if my child is unmotivated or resistant?+
Leon has a coaching background and knows how to work with reluctant students — sometimes a child just needs someone who understands their frustration before they can get to work. That said, there needs to be some baseline coachability: showing up, trying the strategies, and completing some practice. Students who have completely shut down are unlikely to be a good fit.
Is improvement guaranteed?+
No tutor can guarantee results — and Leon won't pretend otherwise. What he can say is that students who follow his strategies consistently and show up with the right attitude typically improve by two grades. The work still has to come from the student.
